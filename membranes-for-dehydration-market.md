<p><p>The dehydration membrane market comprises primarily zeolite membranes and organic membranes. Zeolite membranes utilize crystalline structures to selectively separate water from substances based on size and polarity, offering high permeability and thermal stability. Organic membranes, on the other hand, are composed of polymer materials, providing flexibility and ease of fabrication. They are particularly suited for applications requiring lower temperatures and cost-effectiveness. Both types are integral for improving efficiency in dehydration processes across various industries, including petrochemical and food sectors.</p></p>
