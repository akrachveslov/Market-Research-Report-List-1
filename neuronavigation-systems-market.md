<p><p>Neuronavigation systems are essential in guiding neurosurgical procedures by providing real-time tracking of instruments relative to brain anatomy. Two primary market types are optical systems and electromagnetic systems. Optical systems utilize cameras and markers for precise tracking, offering high accuracy in rigid environments. In contrast, electromagnetic systems rely on magnetic field tracking, allowing for greater flexibility and ease of use in dynamic conditions. Both systems enhance surgical precision and improve patient outcomes by enabling accurate localization of neural structures.</p></p>
