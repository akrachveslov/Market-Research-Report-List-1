<p><p>The 3D implants market includes three primary types: metal, ceramic, and polyethylene. Metal implants, typically made from titanium or stainless steel, offer high strength and durability, making them ideal for load-bearing applications. Ceramic implants are favored for their biocompatibility and aesthetic qualities, often used in dental and orthopedic applications. Polyethylene implants are lightweight and flexible, providing excellent wear resistance, particularly in joint replacements. Each type serves specific medical needs, contributing to advancements in implant technology and patient outcomes.</p></p>
