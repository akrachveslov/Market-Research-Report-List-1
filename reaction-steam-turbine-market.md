<p><p>The reaction steam turbine market is primarily categorized into axial flow and radial flow types. Axial flow turbines feature a design where steam moves parallel to the turbine shaft, allowing for a compact structure and efficient energy conversion, commonly used in high-capacity applications. Conversely, radial flow turbines direct steam perpendicularly to the shaft, offering improved performance in lower flow rates and varying pressure conditions. Each type serves distinct industrial requirements, influencing their adoption across different sectors in the energy landscape.</p></p>
