<p><p>The hydrogels for tissue engineering market comprises two main types: natural and synthetic hydrogels. Natural hydrogels, derived from biological sources like alginate, collagen, and chitosan, offer biocompatibility and mimic the extracellular matrix, promoting cell adhesion and growth. Synthetic hydrogels, created through chemical processes, provide tunable mechanical properties and biodegradability, allowing for customizable scaffold designs. Both types are utilized in regenerative medicine applications, facilitating tissue repair and regeneration by providing a supportive environment for cell proliferation and tissue formation.</p></p>
