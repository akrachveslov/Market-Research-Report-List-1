<p><p>The superconducting quantum interference devices (SQUIDs) market comprises two main types: AC and RF. AC SQUIDs operate by detecting changes in magnetic fields through alternating currents, making them ideal for low-frequency applications like magnetometry. RF SQUIDs, on the other hand, utilize radio frequency signals to measure magnetic fields, offering higher sensitivity and faster response for applications in quantum computing and advanced sensing technologies. Both types leverage the unique properties of superconductors to enhance measurement precision and capabilities in various fields.</p></p>
