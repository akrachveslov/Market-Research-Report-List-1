<p><p>The heat-transfer fluid market comprises two primary types: synthetic organic heat transfer fluids and mineral oils. Synthetic organic fluids are engineered for high thermal stability and efficiency, making them suitable for extreme temperatures and demanding applications. They often exhibit lower viscosity and better heat transfer properties. In contrast, mineral oils are derived from petroleum and are commonly used due to their natural availability and cost-effectiveness. However, they may have limitations in thermal stability and operational temperature ranges compared to synthetic options.</p></p>
