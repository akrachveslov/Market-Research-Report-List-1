<p><ul><li>Mass Spectrometry (MS)</li><li>High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC)</li><li>Capillary Electrophoresis (CE)</li><li>Isoelectric Focusing (IEF) Polyacrylamide Gel (PAGE) Electrophoresis</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The glycan analysis market encompasses various techniques to study carbohydrates attached to proteins. Mass Spectrometry (MS) offers precise molecular weight measurements and structure elucidation. High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC) separates glycan mixtures, providing detailed profiles. Capillary Electrophoresis (CE) allows for rapid analysis based on charge-to-size ratios. Isoelectric Focusing (IEF) separates glycans by isoelectric points, while Polyacrylamide Gel Electrophoresis (PAGE) provides a gel-based platform for size-dependent separations, enhancing the understanding of glycan structures and functions in biological processes.</p></p>
