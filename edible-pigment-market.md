<p><p>The edible pigment market is primarily divided into two types: natural and synthetic pigments. Natural edible pigments are derived from plant, animal, or mineral sources, offering a safer and healthier alternative for coloration in food products and beverages. Examples include beetroot red and turmeric yellow. In contrast, synthetic edible pigments are chemically manufactured and provide a wider range of vibrant colors. They are often more stable and cost-effective but may raise concerns regarding safety and health impacts.</p></p>
